附录：术语索引

本索引收录全书核心术语，按首字母排列。每个术语给出中文名、英文名、简要定义和首次出现的章节。

A

AI 幻觉 / AI Hallucination AI 生成看似正确但实际错误的内容，且不自知其错误。这是 AI 缺乏元认知的典型表现。→ 第 2 章

B

布鲁姆分类法 / Bloom's Taxonomy 教育心理学的认知层级模型，由低到高为：记忆、理解、应用、分析、评估、创造。AI 擅长前三层，人类在后两层仍有优势。→ 第 5 章

C

策略 / Strategy（元认知要素） Flavell 元认知四要素之一。为实现目标而选择的认知策略，元认知水平高的人能灵活切换策略。→ 第 1 章

D

达克效应 / Dunning-Kruger Effect Kruger & Dunning (1999) 发现的认知偏差：能力不足者系统性高估自身水平，因为他们缺乏识别自身无能的元认知能力。→ 第 2 章

第一性原理 / First Principles Thinking 移除所有假设，从最基本的真理出发推理的思维方式。→ 第 3 章

F

反思性实践 / Reflective Practice Schön (1983) 提出的概念：专业人士通过在独特情境中反思（而非机械应用规则）来创造新知识。本质上是跨域的元认知活动。→ 第 7 章

G

归因模糊 / Attribution Ambiguity 在人机协作中，难以清晰区分"自己的贡献"与"AI 的贡献"，导致自我评估失准。→ 第 1 章

J

觉察 / Awareness（元认知支柱） 元认知操作系统四大支柱之一。实时观察自己正在进行的思维过程："我现在在想什么？这是系统 1 还是系统 2？"→ 第 8 章

判断力鸿沟 / Judgment Gap AI 在优化和预测方面表现出色，但在涉及价值判断的决策中存在根本性缺陷——它不能决定"应该"做什么。→ 第 6 章

K

刻意练习 / Deliberate Practice Ericsson 提出的概念：在能力边缘反复练习，配合即时反馈。AI 可以提供反馈，但如果同时完成了工作本身，就没有"边缘"可推。→ 第 5 章

跨域连接 / Cross-Domain Synthesis 将不同领域的知识、经验和洞见联系起来，产生新的理解或创新。人类心智的类比和隐喻能力是跨域连接的基础。→ 第 7 章

L

流畅性幻觉 / Fluency Illusion AI 生成的文本流畅连贯，让使用者误以为自己理解了内容或在掌控产出，实际上可能只是在"编辑"而非"创造"。→ 第 1 章

类比推理 / Analogical Reasoning 通过发现不同事物之间的结构相似性来理解新问题。Hofstadter 称之为"思考的燃料与火焰"。→ 第 7 章

M

MECE 原则 / MECE Principle 相互独立、完全穷尽（Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive）。来自麦肯锡的问题分解框架，用于确保提问的完整性。→ 第 3 章

目标 / Goals（元认知要素） Flavell 元认知四要素之一。当前认知活动的目标，决定了你的监控标准和"够不够"的判断。→ 第 1 章

P

评估 / Evaluation（元认知支柱） 元认知操作系统四大支柱之一。在事后审视思维质量："我的判断准确吗？我的信心是否校准？"→ 第 8 章

Q

期望困难 / Desirable Difficulties Bjork (1994) 提出：使学习过程更困难的因素（如间隔、交错、自测）反而促进长期记忆和迁移。AI 捷径消除了这些困难，也消除了学习效果。→ 第 5 章

R

认知分化 / Cognitive Divergence 本书第 9 章提出的 2035 年情景之一：投资元认知的人变得更强大，不投资的人变得认知依赖，形成新型不平等。→ 第 9 章

S

深度工作 / Deep Work Newport (2016) 提出的概念：在无干扰状态下进行高认知负荷任务。AI 时代的关键不仅是屏蔽干扰，还要知道什么值得深度思考。→ 第 4 章

双重联想 / Bisociation Koestler (1964) 提出：创造性洞察发生在两个原本独立的"思维母体"突然交汇的时刻。→ 第 7 章

T

T 型心智 / T-Shaped Mind 纵向的深度专长加横向的广泛涉猎。AI 延伸纵向深度，只有人类能拓展横向广度；创新发生在交汇处。→ 第 7 章

调节 / Regulation（元认知支柱） 元认知操作系统四大支柱之一。实时调整认知策略："这个方法不奏效了，我该换一种思路。"→ 第 8 章

W

委托陷阱 / Delegation Trap AI 准确率高 → 人类信任增加 → 监督减少 → 少数错误变成灾难性失误的恶性循环。→ 第 6 章

X

系统 1 / 系统 2 / System 1 / System 2 Kahneman (2011) 的双系统理论。系统 1 快速、自动、直觉；系统 2 缓慢、刻意、理性。AI 让系统 1 思维变得更舒适，可能绕过系统 2 的纠错。→ 第 4 章

校准 / Calibration（元认知） 对自身表现水平的估计有多准确。校准良好 = 你的信心水平与实际准确率相匹配。→ 第 2 章

选择性无知 / Selective Ignorance 主动选择不消费可获得的信息。在 AI 生成无限内容的时代，知道什么不需要知道本身是一种元认知技能。→ 第 4 章

Y

愿力 / — → 见"元认知四要素"中的"目标"

元认知 / Metacognition 对认知的认知——对自己思维过程的觉察、监控与调节。由 Flavell (1979) 系统阐述。本书的核心概念。→ 第 1 章

元认知操作系统 / Metacognitive OS 本书第 8 章提出的个人实践框架，由四大支柱组成：觉察、调节、评估、规划。→ 第 8 章

元认知校准 / Metacognitive Calibration 对自身认知状态（知道什么、不知道什么、信心水平）的估计准确度。Shariati et al. (2025) 发现使用 AI 会恶化元认知校准。→ 第 1、2 章

元认知时刻 / Metacognitive Moment 本书每章末尾的反思练习环节，邀请读者暂停阅读，觉察和审视自己的思维过程。→ 全书

元认知体验 / Metacognitive Experience Flavell 元认知四要素之一。认知过程中产生的情绪和感觉（如"卡住了""不太对劲"），是元认知监控的重要信号。→ 第 1 章

元认知知识 / Metacognitive Knowledge Flavell 元认知四要素之一。关于认知任务、认知主体和认知策略的知识。→ 第 1 章

Z

注意力分配框架 / Attention Allocation Framework 本书第 4 章提出的四象限模型：创造（Creating）、评估（Evaluating）、消费（Consuming）、反应（Reacting）。用于审计个人注意力去向。→ 第 4 章

自我调节学习 / Self-Regulated Learning Zimmerman (2002) 的三阶段循环：预思（Forethought）→ 执行（Performance）→ 自我反思（Self-Reflection）。AI 可辅助每个阶段，但学习者需保持元认知主导。→ 第 5 章

Appendix: Glossary

This glossary collects core terms used throughout the book, listed alphabetically. Each entry provides the English term, Chinese equivalent, a brief definition, and the chapter where it first appears.

A

AI Hallucination / AI 幻觉 When AI generates confident but factually incorrect content without recognizing its own error. A classic manifestation of AI's lack of metacognition. → Chapter 2

Analogical Reasoning / 类比推理 Understanding new problems by discovering structural similarities between different things. Hofstadter calls it "the fuel and fire of thinking." → Chapter 7

Attention Allocation Framework / 注意力分配框架 The four-quadrant model proposed in Chapter 4: Creating, Evaluating, Consuming, Reacting. Used to audit where personal attention goes. → Chapter 4

Attribution Ambiguity / 归因模糊 In human-AI collaboration, the difficulty of clearly distinguishing "my contribution" from "AI's contribution," leading to distorted self-assessment. → Chapter 1

Awareness / 觉察 (Metacognitive Pillar) One of four pillars of the Metacognitive OS. Real-time observation of your own thinking: "What am I thinking right now? Is this System 1 or System 2?" → Chapter 8

B

Bisociation / 双重联想 Koestler's (1964) concept: creative insight occurs when two previously independent "matrices of thought" suddenly intersect. → Chapter 7

Bloom's Taxonomy / 布鲁姆分类法 A cognitive hierarchy in educational psychology: Remember → Understand → Apply → Analyze → Evaluate → Create. AI handles the first three well; humans retain advantage in the last two. → Chapter 5

C

Calibration / 校准 (Metacognitive) How accurately you estimate your own performance level. Good calibration = your confidence tracks your actual accuracy. → Chapter 2

Cognitive Divergence / 认知分化 One of three 2035 scenarios proposed in Chapter 9: those who invest in metacognition grow more capable; those who don't become cognitively dependent on AI, creating a new form of inequality. → Chapter 9

Cross-Domain Synthesis / 跨域连接 Connecting knowledge, experience, and insights across different fields to generate new understanding or innovation. Human analogy and metaphor capabilities are the foundation. → Chapter 7

D

Deep Work / 深度工作 Newport's (2016) concept: performing cognitively demanding tasks in a distraction-free state. In the AI era, the key is not just blocking distractions but knowing what deserves deep thought. → Chapter 4

Delegation Trap / 委托陷阱 A vicious cycle: high AI accuracy → increased human trust → reduced oversight → rare errors become catastrophic failures. → Chapter 6

Deliberate Practice / 刻意练习 Ericsson's concept: repeatedly practicing at the edge of your ability with immediate feedback. AI can provide feedback, but if it also does the work, there's no edge to push against. → Chapter 5

Desirable Difficulties / 期望困难 Bjork (1994): factors that make learning harder (spacing, interleaving, self-testing) actually promote long-term retention and transfer. AI shortcuts eliminate these difficulties—and the learning. → Chapter 5

Dunning-Kruger Effect / 达克效应 Cognitive bias discovered by Kruger & Dunning (1999): the incompetent systematically overestimate their own competence because they lack the metacognitive ability to recognize their incompetence. → Chapter 2

E

Evaluation / 评估 (Metacognitive Pillar) One of four pillars of the Metacognitive OS. Post-hoc assessment of thinking quality: "Was my judgment accurate? Was my confidence calibrated?" → Chapter 8

F

First Principles Thinking / 第一性原理 Reasoning from the most fundamental truths after removing all assumptions. → Chapter 3

Fluency Illusion / 流畅性幻觉 AI-generated text is so fluent and coherent that users mistake it for understanding or mastery, when they may only be "editing" rather than "creating." → Chapter 1

G

Goals / 目标 (Metacognitive Element) One of Flavell's four metacognitive elements. The objective of your current cognitive activity, which determines your monitoring standards and "good enough" judgments. → Chapter 1

J

Judgment Gap / 判断力鸿沟 AI excels at optimization and prediction but has a fundamental deficit in value-laden decisions—it cannot decide what "should" happen. → Chapter 6

M

MECE Principle / MECE 原则 Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive. McKinsey's problem decomposition framework, used to ensure completeness in questioning. → Chapter 3

Metacognition / 元认知 Cognition about cognition—awareness, monitoring, and regulation of your own thinking processes. Systematically described by Flavell (1979). The core concept of this book. → Chapter 1

Metacognitive Calibration / 元认知校准 The accuracy of your estimates about your own cognitive state (what you know, don't know, confidence levels). Shariati et al. (2025) found AI use worsens metacognitive calibration. → Chapters 1, 2

Metacognitive Experience / 元认知体验 One of Flavell's four elements. Emotions and feelings arising during cognitive processes ("I'm stuck," "something feels off"), serving as important metacognitive monitoring signals. → Chapter 1

Metacognitive Knowledge / 元认知知识 One of Flavell's four elements. Knowledge about cognitive tasks, cognitive agents, and cognitive strategies. → Chapter 1

Metacognitive Moment / 元认知时刻 The reflection exercise section at the end of each chapter, inviting readers to pause and examine their own thinking processes. → All chapters

Metacognitive OS / 元认知操作系统 The personal practice framework proposed in Chapter 8, built on four pillars: Awareness, Regulation, Evaluation, Planning. → Chapter 8

P

Planning / 规划 (Metacognitive Pillar) One of four pillars of the Metacognitive OS. Designing your cognitive approach before starting: "What kind of thinking does this problem require?" → Chapter 8

R

Reflective Practice / 反思性实践 Schön's (1983) concept: professionals create new knowledge by reflecting in unique situations rather than mechanically applying rules. Inherently cross-domain metacognitive activity. → Chapter 7

Regulation / 调节 (Metacognitive Pillar) One of four pillars of the Metacognitive OS. Real-time adjustment of cognitive strategy: "This approach isn't working. I should switch to a different angle." → Chapter 8

S

Selective Ignorance / 选择性无知 Deliberately choosing not to consume available information. In an era of AI-generated infinite content, knowing what you don't need to know is itself a metacognitive skill. → Chapter 4

Self-Regulated Learning / 自我调节学习 Zimmerman's (2002) three-phase cycle: Forethought → Performance → Self-Reflection. AI can support each phase, but the learner must maintain metacognitive control. → Chapter 5

Strategy / 策略 (Metacognitive Element) One of Flavell's four elements. Cognitive strategies chosen to achieve goals; metacognitively skilled individuals flexibly switch strategies based on feedback. → Chapter 1

System 1 / System 2 / 系统 1 / 系统 2 Kahneman's (2011) dual-process theory. System 1: fast, automatic, intuitive. System 2: slow, deliberate, rational. AI makes System 1 thinking dangerously comfortable, potentially bypassing System 2's error-checking. → Chapter 4

T

T-Shaped Mind / T 型心智 Deep expertise vertically plus broad awareness horizontally. AI extends your vertical depth; only you can extend horizontal breadth. Innovation happens at the intersections. → Chapter 7
